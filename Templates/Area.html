Did You know that depending upon the type of a triangle, you can find the area with a different specific formula?

Equilateral Triangle

Area of Equilateral Triangle can be calculated with the formula (√3)/4 × side2

Isoceles Traingle

When an equal side and the base is given. Area of an isosceles triangle = 1/4 × b√4a2-b2 where 'b' is the base and 'a' is the measure of equal side.
Also, In case you don't know what kind of triangle it is, and you do not have the height of the triangle either, We can find the area with the help of sides only!

Heron's Formula

When the sides of a triangle are given as a, b, and c. (Heron's formula) Area of a scalene triangle = √s(s− a)(s-b)(s-c) where a, b, and c are the sides and 's' is the semi-perimeter; s = (a + b + c)/2
